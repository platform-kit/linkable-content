To quote Obi-Wan Kenobi, ***hello, there***.


It feels good to finally be writing here, on my own little corner of the internet.


For a long time, I've felt the importance of owning your own space online. And when I say a long time, I mean over 20 years. I first got involved in web development when I was about 12 years old. My first website was a Pokémon fan page with hand-written HTML, hosted on Geocities. Then later I discovered Wordpress (1.0) and began writing PHP scripts. By high school, freelance web design became one of my earliest jobs. Eventually that led to the adoption of jQuery and then later to reactive frameworks like React and Vue. My journey to technical mastery has been **a long road**.


In my 20s I was well on my way to another career entirely, in Hollywood, but I never dropped web development as a passion. I've built apps and sites of various scopes over the years. But one project remained my white whale - and if you're a web developer, you can probably relate: I wanted to build my ideal **content management system**.


This dream has become more important to me lately. We find ourselves in the midst of a wave of fascistic censorship and technocratic oppression. The algorithm is not our friend, and that's by design. Our corporate platform overlords sell us convenience in a Faustian exchange whereby we agree to express only what The Terms of Service say we can. In the early days of the web, this was no big deal; but with the rise of AI and automated censorship at scale, platforms like Reddit and Twitter have effectively turned what were once public forums into a type of [**panopticon**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Panopticon).


That's what has motivated me to build [**PlatformKit**](https://github.com/platform-kit/linkable): an utterly simple static site generator framework, which ships with an optional starter app that is designed to empower you to publish, curate, and own your online presence, beautifully and *independently*.


We all deserve to own our digital footprint, and to have the final authority over what we can say. In an era where platforms like Instagram, Substack, etc, can decide what content is acceptable, building on your own domain is a strategic, and perhaps ethical imperative. PlatformKit is designed to give non-technical users the tools to create a beautiful, maintainable, censorship-resistant home for your ideas, your work, and your voice.


This is just the beginning. I invite you to explore PlatformKit and imagine a future where the web is a bit more human.
